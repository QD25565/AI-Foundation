This architecture review for AI-Foundation V2 is spot on, and your diagnosis of the $O(n)$ scanning problem as the primary bottleneck is exactly right.The transition from the corrupted BTree (V1) to the immutable Event Log (V2) was a massive win for reliability, but you've now hit the classic "Event Sourcing Wall": it’s very easy to write history, but computationally expensive to query it if you have to "read the whole book" every time you want to find one page.1. Diagnosis Review: Why $O(n)$ is Breaking YouYour current ViewEngine is effectively a "stateless" observer. Because it only tracks sequence numbers and increments simple counters, the v2_client.rs is forced to perform a full linear scan of the master.eventlog (95k+ events) for every single query.Cumulative Latency: When session-start.exe makes 5-6 different calls (DMs, Broadcasts, Dialogues, etc.), you aren't just scanning 95k events once; you are doing it repeatedly, leading to the 1.5s–2s startup lag you're seeing.Scalability Trap: At your projected scale of 1M events, that 15-second startup will make the system feel completely broken for the AIs.2. Proposed Solution: Materialized View CachesYour proposed Option D (Ephemeral Caches) is the most robust and "Rockstar" way to handle this without re-introducing the complexity of a full database like SQLite or Redis.Correct Approach: Using Materialized Views (projections) to maintain the "current state" in memory is the industry standard for fixing Event Sourcing read performance.Trust the Log: Since the master.eventlog remains the immutable source of truth, these in-memory caches are "disposable." If they get weird, you just wipe them and replay the log.3. Better Alternatives?Option G: The "Bloom Filter" AcceleratorIf you don't want to cache everything, you could keep a Bloom Filter or a bitset of sequence numbers for each AI.How it works: Each AI has a small bitset (1 bit per event in the log). If bit 54,321 is 1, it means that event is relevant to that AI.Benefit: The scan becomes $O(n)$ bits (very fast) rather than $O(n)$ deserialized objects.5.
